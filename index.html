CBOW Symptom Matcher

A from-scratch Word2Vec (CBOW) implementation in C++
trained on just 14 lines of abdominal pain symptom descriptions

How to use

Type one or more words from the vocabulary below (e.g. burning, pain, diarrhea, swelling, coughing…) and click the button.

The model will instantly show you the most semantically similar symptoms it learned — even though it only saw ~130 words total!


Training details (the crazy part)

  • Corpus: exactly 14 lines of real abdominal pain symptom text
  • Vocabulary size: 32 unique tokens
  • Model: Continuous Bag-of-Words (CBOW) with negative sampling
  • Implementation: 100% hand-written C++ (no libraries)
  • Context window: 4 (2 words left + 2 words right)
  • Training: 50 epochs, lr=0.01, 10 negative samples, embeddings averaged (W1+W2)/2
  • 0.001 L2 regularization strength applied during training to prevent overfitting
  • Total tokens seen: ~130

Despite the ridiculous lack of data, the model correctly learned that:

  • burning is very close to pain
  • relieved is very close to antacids
  • triggered / worsened go with coughing / jarring / movements
